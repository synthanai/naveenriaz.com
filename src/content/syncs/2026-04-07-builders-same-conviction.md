I spent 70 minutes with a small AI product firm building exactly what I believed in privately.

Not the same words. Not the same pitch. The same structural conviction: generic AI is a dead end.

They had built domain-specific models deployed on client hardware. No cloud dependency. Data stays local. The client owns the AI, not the vendor. They had sold one to a lens company. Full exit. The cycle was complete: identify the domain problem, build for it, hand it over.

I do not use the word "alignment" loosely. But this was alignment.

The thing that struck me most was not the product logic. It was the sales gap. Here were two technically strong parties, both confessing that the hardest part of building something real is convincing the first stranger to trust you enough to try it. Neither of us is built for cold outreach. Neither of us pretends to be.

What we are built for is proximity. Small projects. Real delivery. Track record as the only currency that doesn't depreciate.

We agreed, without saying it explicitly, that the right model is not to grow sales. It is to grow trust until sales become unnecessary.

The conversation ended with a WhatsApp group nobody had created yet. That is either the beginning of something or a polite full stop. The difference is whether one of us sends the first message.

I intend to be that person.
