linkedin_post: |
  Stop building advanced AI agents without asking the most critical question.
  
  You know dozens of reasoning frameworks: Chain-of-Thought, Tree-of-Thought, Graph-of-Thought. The field has spent years optimizing *how* an LLM thinks.
  
  The overlooked problem is *why*.
  
  Right now, selecting the right reasoning structure is often guesswork. We pick a model because the original paper used it, or because it sounds sophisticated.
  
  This is like choosing a surgical tool before diagnosing the patient. We are obsessed with topology, ignoring purpose.
  
  The fundamental gap is this: step-level intent exists, but topology-level intent governance does not.
  
  We need a "Purpose" checkpoint before selecting the "Structure."
  
  This is the Intent of Thought (IoT) framework. It forces three checks:
  
  *   **Purpose:** What must the AI achieve?
  *   **Anti-Purpose:** What specific failure mode must we avoid?
  *   **Success Signal:** How do we prove the reasoning worked?
  
  If the purpose is mapping causal factors, a Graph-of-Thought is required. A simple sequential proof demands a Chain-of-Thought. The purpose dictates the structure.
  
  Stop asking, "How should we reason?" Start asking, "What are we trying to achieve?"
  
  By making this shift, you prevent hours of misaligned, complex reasoning. Purpose is the missing variable in the entire AI architecture stack.

Gartner's 2022 research: employees' willingness to support enterprise change dropped from 74% in 2016 to 43%. Not because people hate change. Because they have been through too many changes that led nowhere.

The third restructuring in two years. The new tool that replaced the last new tool. The strategy pivot that contradicted last quarter's strategy pivot. Each one demanded emotional investment. None returned dividends.

Change fatigue is not resistance. It is rational self-preservation. The team has learned that investing energy in the latest initiative is a losing bet.

Before launching the next transformation, finish the last one.
