As an organisation grows, the sheer gravity of its scale begins to distort its original architecture.

The temptation to build simply because you have the resources to build is immense. But taking on complex mass without a structural core guarantees collapse.

Growth is a vector. It needs both magnitude and direction. Magnitude alone is a catastrophe waiting for a catalyst.

Motion is not direction. Scale without purpose is just acceleration towards chaos.

Is your growth compromising your core identity and creating operational debt?
