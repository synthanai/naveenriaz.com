The commonly accepted lie is that preserving a founder's original systems is the best way to honour their legacy.

But here is why it fails:
Every system designed by a founder was an answer to a specific environment. When the environment changes, preserving the original answer becomes the fastest way to fail the original intent.

There is a difference between stewardship and submission. A ghost cannot make decisions.

Do not ask what they would do. Ask what problem they were trying to solve.

Are you leading, or simply embalming the past?
