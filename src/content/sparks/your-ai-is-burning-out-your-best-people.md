Here is a pattern nobody is discussing.

Companies adopt AI to reduce workload. Initially, it works. Drafts arrive faster. Research summarises itself. Reports write themselves.

Then the burden shifts. Every draft needs verification. Every summary needs fact-checking. Every report needs a human to confirm the AI did not hallucinate. The cognitive load does not decrease. It transforms from creation to verification.

Past a threshold of AI tools, productivity does not just plateau. It reverses. More tools create more decisions, more context-switching, more oversight fatigue.

The industry predicted this phenomenon. We called it Enthusiasm-Driven Compromise: the pattern where excitement about a new capability erodes the governance needed to use it well.

The fix is not fewer tools. It is better boundaries.
