Walk into any corporate lobby and you will find them. Framed on the wall, printed in a clean font. "Integrity. Innovation. Collaboration. Excellence." They look beautiful.

Now walk into the same company's Monday meeting. Does the person who raised a concern last week get thanked or side-eyed? Does a failed experiment earn curiosity or blame? Does the junior employee's idea get the same airtime as the VP's?

That is where the real values live. Not in the frame. In the hallway.

Patrick Lencioni called these "aspirational values" versus "actual values." Every organisation has both. The aspirational ones go on the poster. The actual ones govern what happens when someone makes a mistake, disagrees with leadership, or asks an uncomfortable question.

The test is simple. When "integrity" is on the wall but the team watches a leader bend the rules without consequence, the wall becomes wallpaper. The team reads the behaviour, not the banner.

Culture is not what you declare. It is what you tolerate.
