"We do it this way because that is how the founder set it up."

Every established company has a version of this sentence. It is spoken with reverence, as if the original decision was carved in stone by someone who could see the future. But the person who built the company in 1998 did not know about cloud computing, remote work, or the fact that the market would look completely different by 2026.

There is a difference between honouring a legacy and being trapped by one. Honouring means understanding the intent behind the decision and adapting it to new conditions. Being trapped means preserving the exact form of a decision whose context has disappeared.

Robert Iger, returning to Disney, faced exactly this. Walt Disney's creative vision was sacred, but the operational structures built in a different era needed to evolve. The purpose stayed. The methods changed.

Every generation of a team needs to reclaim the purpose as their own. Not reject the founder, not worship the founder. Translate the founder.

The ghost does not have the keys. You do.
