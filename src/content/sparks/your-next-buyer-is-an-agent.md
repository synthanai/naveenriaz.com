Nyk wrote a provocation: "Your next buyer won't fill out a form. It will hit an MCP endpoint with a budget."

He is describing a world where AI agents act as purchasing agents. They evaluate products, compare alternatives, negotiate terms, and execute transactions. No human in the loop. No landing page. No demo call.

The sales funnel collapses into a single API call.

This sounds radical until you notice that MCP (Model Context Protocol) already enables agents to discover and invoke external tools programmatically. The infrastructure exists. The agents are learning to use it.

The question is not whether agent commerce will arrive. It is whether your product has an endpoint.
