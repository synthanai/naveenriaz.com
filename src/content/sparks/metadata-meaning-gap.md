A search engine finds documents that contain the word "trust." A knowledge system understands that the trust problem in the engineering team is connected to the hiring decision made six months ago and the feedback culture that was never established.

That gap, between finding and understanding, is the metadata-to-meaning gap. And it is where most "AI-powered knowledge" systems fall short.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) has become the default architecture for enterprise AI. But most implementations are just search engines with a language model on top. They retrieve documents by keyword similarity. They do not understand the relationships between ideas, the evolution of a concept over time, or the context that makes one piece of information relevant and another irrelevant.

We built a system that tracks not just what was written, but why it was written and how it connects to everything else. The difference is dramatic. The same query produces a paragraph of context instead of a pile of documents.

Stop building smarter search. Start building systems that understand the connections between the things they find.
