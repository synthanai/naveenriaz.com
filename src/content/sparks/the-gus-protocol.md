# The Gus Protocol

Networking fails when it is transactional.

We try to build networks when we try to not build friendships.
We tend to do it from the getting side and not from the giving side.

True influence is built by providing value first without expectation.

I call this the Gus Protocol.

It is assuming the identity of a serial connector:
One who identifies the resonance between disparate entities
And bridges them without expectation.

Good people are interesting people.
Great people are interested people.

Shift from performing (being interesting)
To observing and empowering (being interested).

If you want to build social capital,
you have to give value that creates a vacuum for growth.

You grow to give; you give to grow.

This is the law of the Transmuter.
