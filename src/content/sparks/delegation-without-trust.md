"I trust you completely. Just send me the draft before you share it. And CC me on the emails. And check with me before the meeting."

That is not delegation. That is remote-controlled execution with extra steps.

A manager thinks they are being responsible. The employee experiences something different: a ceiling dressed up as a doorway. Every "just check with me" erodes a little more of the ownership that delegation is supposed to create.

Liz Wiseman, in her research on Multipliers, found that the best leaders gave people challenges that stretched them and then got out of the way. The worst leaders were "Diminishers" who maintained control disguised as helpfulness. Both types believed they were doing the right thing. The difference was visible only to the person underneath.

Trust is a risk. There is no version of delegation without it. If you cannot tolerate the possibility that they will do it differently from you (or even make a mistake), you have not delegated. You have just distributed your anxiety.

Let go of the wheel. That is where growth lives.
