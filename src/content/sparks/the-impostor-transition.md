# The Impostor Transition

I have always had Imposter Syndrome. Even now I am facing the same after getting promoted to CTO.

Impostor syndrome is not a psychological flaw.

It is a transition symptom.

It is what happens when your identity shifts but your surroundings have not caught up.
It is the gap between who you were and who you are becoming.
It is your nervous system running an older version of you in a newer environment.

We call this growth!

But here is the trap:

We often confuse the discomfort of transition
with evidence that we do not belong.

Feeling uncertain is not feeling incompetent.
Feeling out of place is not being out of place.

Just feeling like a fraud is not being a fraud!

Every time I have stepped into a room where I had no business being,
that friction was the exact proof I was moving in the right direction.

If you want to grow beyond where you are,
you have to survive the middle ocean.

Normalize the doubt; It means you are no longer where you were.
