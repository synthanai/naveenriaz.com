A CTO fixes the deployment pipeline. Three months later, the incident rate drops. Six months later, developer morale improves. Nobody connects the two.

Organisational problems are not independent items on a whiteboard. They are threads in a loom. Pull one and three others move. Ignore one and three others tighten.

We built a system that tracks 108 organisational patterns and maps their connections. The finding that keeps recurring: most "new" problems are downstream symptoms of an upstream knot that was never untangled.

Before starting the next initiative, trace the thread backwards. The problem you are about to solve may already be solved if you fix the one above it.
