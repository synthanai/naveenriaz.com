A warehouse team is exhausted. Customer complaints are rising. Marketing blames product. Product blames engineering. Everyone treats these as separate fires.

But they are not separate. They are threads in the same weave. The warehouse team is exhausted because a process change six months ago doubled their workload without adjusting headcount. The process change happened because engineering shipped a new feature without consulting operations. The feature was rushed because marketing promised a launch date without checking capacity.

One thread, five symptoms.

We call this the LOOM insight: most organisational problems are not isolated. They are woven. Pull one thread and three others move. Treat each symptom independently and you spend your entire budget putting out fires that keep relighting from the same source.

The skill that matters most in the next decade is not firefighting. It is pattern connection: the ability to look across functions and see how a trust problem in the warehouse is creating a revenue problem on the website.

Stop fixing threads. Start reading the weave.
