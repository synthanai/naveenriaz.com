We started a bakery to feed the neighbourhood. The bread was warm. People queued. The purpose was visible in every loaf.

Then we grew. We hired a logistics manager. Then a supply chain analyst. Then a digital marketing team. Somewhere between the third warehouse and the second CRM platform, we forgot we were a bakery. Now we optimise delivery routes for bread that arrives cold.

This is purpose amnesia. Not a dramatic crisis, just a slow drift. The "how" gets so sophisticated that it eats the "why" for breakfast.

Simon Sinek built his entire framework around this diagnosis: organisations that lose connection to their "why" become commodities competing on cost and efficiency alone. But efficiency without purpose is just a faster route to a destination nobody cares about.

The fix is not a rebrand or a new mission statement. It is a question asked regularly, out loud, in rooms where the answer matters: "What problem did we exist to solve, and are we still solving it?"

Go back to the kitchen. Find the smell of the bread.
