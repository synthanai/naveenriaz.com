A factory floor has a quality issue. A marketing campaign underperforms. A customer support queue is growing. Three teams, three problems, three budgets, three consultants.

But the quality issue exists because a process change was made without consulting the floor team. The marketing campaign underperforms because the product promise no longer matches the product reality. The support queue grows because neither the factory nor the marketing team is talking to each other.

One thread. Three symptoms. The person who can see the connection is worth more than the three specialists combined.

In the AI era, the "how" is increasingly free. AI can write the code, draft the report, generate the analysis. What AI cannot do (yet) is look across the loom and see how disparate threads connect into a pattern.

That is the Pattern Connector: a role defined not by domain expertise but by cross-domain vision. The ability to see how a decision in one corner of the organisation creates a consequence in another.

Anyone can see the thread. Few can see the weave. The future belongs to the weavers.
