# Heimskur: The Idiot at Home

Legend holds that Icelandic Vikings did not just sail south to plunder gold and silver; they sailed to steal ideas. How do they farm in Denmark? How do they build ships in Norway? While taking physical treasure was important, stealing systemic patterns was essential for survival. If a Viking refused to journey into the world for new inspiration, they were labeled "heimskur." This word translates directly to idiot, but its root "heim" literally means home. An idiot is simply someone who never leaves their home. True intelligence requires hunting across borders for knowledge that works. SYNTHAI captures this exact ethos within the STEAL methodology every single morning.
