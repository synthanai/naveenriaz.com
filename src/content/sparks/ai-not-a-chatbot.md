Most organisations think "AI adoption" means adding a chatbot to their website. A text box. A prompt. A response. That is the demo, not the product.

The real product is the harness: the infrastructure that wraps around the AI and makes it useful. State management. Memory across sessions. Retry logic when models fail. Context injection so the AI knows who it is talking to and why. Guardrails so it does not hallucinate its way into a compliance violation.

We spent six months building an agentic system. Less than 10% of the effort went into the AI model itself. The other 90% was plumbing: how the agent remembers context, how it escalates when uncertain, how it audits its own decisions, how it fails gracefully instead of confidently.

An AI without a harness is a loud guest who forgets the conversation halfway through dinner. An AI with a harness is a colleague who shows up prepared.

Stop prompt-tuning. Start building the infrastructure that makes the prompt matter.
