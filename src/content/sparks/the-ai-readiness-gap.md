McKinsey's 2024 State of AI report found that 72% of organisations have adopted AI in at least one function. But only 26% have scaled it beyond pilot projects. That is a 46-point gap between acquisition and absorption.

The pattern is familiar. A leadership team approves an AI budget. A vendor is selected. A pilot is launched. Early results look promising. Then the pilot ends and nothing changes. The model sits unused. The data pipeline rots. The champion moves to another role.

The gap is never technical. The models work. The APIs are stable. The gap is organisational: unclear ownership, missing governance, no feedback loop between the tool and the workflow it is supposed to transform.

AI readiness is not about having the technology. It is about having the organisational muscle to metabolise it. Most companies have purchased a gym membership. Very few have started training.
